"Hope is a discipline" - Mariame Kaba



What is abolition?

Abolition is the eradication of the prison-industrial complex and other structures of violence that disproportionately target people of color. Abolition seeks to create a new world through restorative and transformative justice practices.

Why abolition? The system is not broken!

The current structure of society maintains systemic issues, such as racism and sexism, that marginalize certain groups of people and uphold white, patriarchal values. Due to the inherent structures of institutions, they must be eradicated and new systems must be built. The system is not broken because that implies that it had at one point worked-- the system has never worked and therefore must end. Reforms are not enough.

What can I do as a youth abolitionist?

Abolitionists do not have uniform ideas on how to eradicate the current system. They focus their energy on efforts that they believe are most effective or address a specific aspect of society. Youth abolitionists can get involved through various actions.
