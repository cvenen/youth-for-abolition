What is abolition?

Abolition is the eradication of the prison-industrial complex and other structures of violence that disproportionately target people of color. Abolition seeks to create a new world through restorative and transformative justice practices.

Why abolition? The system is not broken!

Social and political institutions maintain racist and sexist systems that uphold white, patriarchal values and marginalize certain groups of people. Since the inherent structure of these institutions is flawed, they must be abolished and new systems must be built. We can't say the system is broken because that implies it had worked at some point-- the system has never worked and therefore must end. Reforms are not enough.
